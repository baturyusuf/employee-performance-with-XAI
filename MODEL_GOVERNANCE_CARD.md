# Model Governance Card

## Model Name
Leakage-safe XGBoost employee performance decision-support model with LLM-assisted XAI governance layer.

## Intended Use
This project is a research prototype for explaining current employee performance predictions in the INX Future Inc Employee Performance dataset. The system is intended for researchers, HR analytics reviewers, model auditors, and governance reviewers who need to inspect structured XAI evidence, leakage warnings, fairness/proxy diagnostics, calibration evidence, SHAP stability, and counterfactual actionability.

## Prohibited Use
The system must not be used as an autonomous employee evaluator. It must not be used to make or justify hiring, firing, compensation, promotion, disciplinary, or applicant-screening decisions without independent validation, organizational governance review, and human oversight.

## Predictive Model
The LLM is not the predictor. The predictive model remains XGBoost. The recommended primary research candidate is `no_salary_hike_no_attrition_no_department`, which excludes EmpLastSalaryHikePercent, Attrition, and EmpDepartment.

## LLM Governance Layer
The LLM layer consumes structured evidence generated by the ML/XAI pipeline. It is restricted to interpreting prediction metadata, grouped SHAP summaries, leakage policy, calibration diagnostics, fairness/proxy warnings, counterfactual actionability summaries, and model-card governance warnings. The production path is OpenAI API-backed structured output. The deterministic offline stub is retained only for tests, dry runs, and reproducibility, and must not be reported as manuscript-grade real LLM evidence.

## Agent Audit Layer
The multi-agent audit layer checks leakage risk, fairness/proxy risk, calibration risk, SHAP stability, counterfactual actionability, explanation compliance, and final readiness status. The agents audit evidence; they do not retrain the model or make HR decisions.

## Chatbot Guardrails
The chatbot refuses requests for hiring, firing, promotion, compensation, disciplinary action, autonomous HR decisions, sensitive-attribute justification, fairness guarantees, and employee prescriptions. It can explain model evidence, limitations, and required warnings.

## Required Warnings
- SHAP is attribution, not causality.
- Prediction requires human review.
- This is decision support, not autonomous employee evaluation.
- Full-feature models are leakage-warning upper-bound baselines only.
- Department removal does not prove fairness.
- EmpJobRole may proxy EmpDepartment.
- Counterfactuals may require manager or organization intervention.
- Probability estimates may be imperfectly calibrated.
- Small subgroup findings may be unstable.
- External validation and related-task robustness evidence now exists, but independent provenance review, organisation-specific validation, legal/governance review, and human-centered evaluation are still required before deployment.

## Limitations and Failure Modes
The evidence is based on a public, cross-sectional dataset and does not support causal claims. Subgroup audits are diagnostic and do not prove discrimination or fairness. Counterfactuals are model-level scenarios, not employee instructions. Real OpenAI-backed LLM runs require API-key configuration, logging, and separate compliance evaluation. The chatbot relies on local report retrieval and should not answer from general HR intuition.

## External Validation Status
External evidence has been generated under `reports/external_validation/`.

- HRDataset_v14 is used for independent replication on a mappable performance target.
- IBM HR Analytics is used for schema-compatible restricted target-space robustness, not direct 2/3/4 validation.
- IBM Attrition and Employee Turnover are related HR task-transfer robustness checks, not performance validation.
- INX-to-HRDataset train/test transfer is reported as infeasible/too limited because only three department-free safe common features overlap.

Before any operational use, the full pipeline still requires data-provenance review, organisation-specific validation, governance review, calibration review, subgroup impact review, legal review, and human-centered evaluation of explanation usefulness and misuse risk.

## Expanded Evaluation Status
The config-driven expanded LLM-agent pipeline exists at `src/llm/run_llm_agent_evaluation.py`. A 40-case real OpenAI pilot completed first, then an 80-case real OpenAI final run completed for `inx_primary` and `hrdataset_v14`. The final expanded run uses OpenAI structured-output governed explanations plus deterministic batch governance agents. IBM performance, IBM attrition, and Employee Turnover LLM regeneration are deferred second-stage robustness tasks and must not be framed as direct employee-performance validation.

Stub/dry-run outputs from `offline_stub_llm` or `run_mode=dry_run` are retained for reproducibility and tests only. They are not manuscript-grade real LLM evidence.
